QB structure

Teams with both top-2 QBs by R6 made the playoffs 67% of the time (10/15) vs 38% (8/21) — a group holding 3/3 champions and 5/6 finalists. The champion shape is one elite + one solid (2023: R1+R4 · 2024: R2+R2 · 2025: R1+R5), not two premium picks: both R1+R2 double-QB starts finished bottom-two.

Decade check (xlsx boards; same 2QB format all era): only 4 of 11 recorded champions had both QBs by R6 — 2015 donnie R1+R6 ✓ (PF#2) · 2016 kyle_figgy R1+R8 (PF#1) · 2017 coop R9+R10 (PF#1) · 2018 dave_aka_wang R8+R9 (PF#2) · 2019 troy_mullings R4+R12 (PF#1) · 2020 trevor_bergerboy R5+R10 (PF#4) · 2021 dave_aka_wang R3+R7 (PF#1) · 2022 ankur_patel R4+R9 (PF#7) · 2023 eric_m R1+R4 ✓ · 2024 coop R2+R2 ✓ · 2025 trevor_bergerboy R1+R5 ✓ — and three of the four are the Sleeper-era champs above. Read with one confound: Yahoo-era rounds include unflagged keepers at COST, so a champion's "R10 QB2" may be a kept elite at a discount seat — the decade refutes early QB spending as champion law, not QB capital. Together with the outside benchmark (which also fails this signal), treat 2QB-by-R6 as a current-regime exploit of this room's QB-late minority — real, priced, and revocable — not a law of winning. PF is the decade law too: 6 of 8 champions with parsed Yahoo/Sleeper standings finished top-3 in scoring.
